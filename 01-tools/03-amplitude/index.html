Amplitude

Up until now, we have only spoken about two of a wave's three fundamental properties: frequency and phase. However, we now need to look at the third property called amplitude.

On the graph below, point `P` is fixed at 60° relative to when the wave crosses the Y axis. As you change the sliders, notice how the amplitude of point `P` changes in both the positive an negative direction.

Frequency

Frequency describes how many times the wave oscillates up and down within a given period of time.
The standard period of time is 1 second, and the units are cycles per second, or Hz.

Phase

Phase describes where in the up and down cycle some point on a wave has reached.

Amplitude

Amplitude measures the overall height of a wave; in other words, it measures the difference between a wave's positive and negative peaks.
